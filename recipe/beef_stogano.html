Beef Stroganoff

Description

Beef Stroganoff is a classic Russian dish, made with sautéed pieces of beef served in a sauce with sour cream. From its origins in mid-19th-century Russia, it has become popular around the world, with considerable variation from the original recipe. This version of Beef Stroganoff is a perfect family meal—it's easy to make, hearty, and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound - Top Sirloin Steak (thinly sliced into strips)
  • 2 tablespoons - Olive Oil
  • 2 tablespoons - Unsalted Butter
  • ½ - Medium Onion (finely chopped)
  • ½ pound - Brown Mushrooms (thickly sliced)
  • 1 - Garlic Cloves (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon - All-Purpose Flour
  • 1 cup - Beef Broth
  • ¾ cup - Heavy Whipping Cream
  • ¼ cup - Sour Cream
  • 1 tablespoon - Worcestershire Sauce
  • ½ teaspoon - Dijon Mustard
  • ½ teaspoon - Salt
  • ¼ teaspoon - Black Pepper
  • 1 tablespoon - Green Onion (to garnish)
  • 12 ounces - Egg Noodles (to serve)

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the beef strips and cook until browned. Remove the beef from the pan and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the butter. Once melted, add the onion, mushrooms, and garlic. Cook until the onions are translucent and the mushrooms are tender.
  3. Add the flour to the pan and stir to combine. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Slowly whisk in the beef broth, making sure to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream, sour cream, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture back to a simmer.
  6. Return the beef to the pan and simmer until the beef is cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  7. Serve the Beef Stroganoff over egg noodles, garnished with green onion.
